All subcontractors must comply with Builders FirstSource Safety Management Program requirements, including training, PPE usage, hazard reporting, and adherence to all jobsite safety rules.

The Subcontractor is required to perform all work under the Master Agreement in accordance with the Builders FirstSource Safety Management Program.

A. General

  1. Builders FirstSource is committed to promoting a safe work environment for employees, customers, subcontractor employees, representatives, and visitors.
  2. The term ‚Äúemployee‚Äù refers to all employees, personnel, subcontractors, and suppliers present at a Builders FirstSource project site.
  3. The safety and health of every individual on Builders FirstSource jobsites is critical. All parties are responsible for observing safe work practices and avoiding at-risk behavior.
  4. Compliance with all Builders FirstSource safety policies and procedures is required. Nothing in this program diminishes the standard of care.
  5. The Subcontractor is 100% responsible for the safety of its employees, suppliers, and all others for whom they are accountable on site.

B. Roles & Responsibilities

  • Subcontractors must designate a ‚ÄúCompetent Person‚Äù as required by OSHA regulations.
  • All subcontractor employees are responsible for their own safety and compliance with federal, state, and local safety regulations.
  • Employees must report unsafe conditions or hazards immediately.

C. Written Safety Plans

  • When required, subcontractors must develop and maintain a Safety & Health Plan or IIPP.
  • Plans must address hazard assessment, communication, training, compliance, accident investigation, and corrective actions.
  • Fall protection plans are required for work performed at heights of 6 feet or more.

D. Training

  • The subcontractor is responsible for providing all necessary safety training to employees and suppliers.
  • Builders FirstSource may require additional or specialized training as a condition of continued work on a project.

E. Compliance

  • Failure to comply with safety requirements may result in removal from the project, probation, suspension, or termination.
  • OSHA citations involving subcontractors are the responsibility of the subcontractor, including all corrective actions and penalties.

F. Incident Reporting Procedures

  1. Injured employees must be sent for medical treatment immediately when required.
  2. Builders FirstSource Management must be notified immediately by phone.
  3. An incident report must be submitted within 24 hours.
  4. Builders FirstSource reserves the right to investigate all incidents.

G. Communication

  • Subcontractors must maintain Safety Data Sheets (SDS) for hazardous materials.
  • Hazardous materials must be disclosed prior to being brought onto the jobsite.

H. Code of Safe Practices

  • All subcontractor personnel must comply with jobsite safety rules.
  • Housekeeping, walkways, stairwells, and exits must remain clear.
  • Proper PPE is required at all times.
  • Unsafe conditions or behaviors must be corrected immediately.

This section is provided for reference only. Full compliance with the Builders FirstSource Safety Management Program is mandatory.
